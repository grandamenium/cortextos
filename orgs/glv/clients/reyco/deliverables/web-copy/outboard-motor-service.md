# Outboard Motor Service in Sault Ste. Marie

Ice goes out on Lake Superior in late April or early May most years — two to three weeks later than the inland lakes of the Algoma district. For outboard owners in the Sault, that compressed season means every spring hour counts, and a motor that is not ready on opening day is an opening day you miss.

Reyco Marine services outboard motors for customers across the Algoma district. Whether you are running a Mercury on the St. Marys River or heading out to Batchawana Bay, our service team handles everything from seasonal tune-ups to major powerhead work.

---

## What We Service

We service outboard motors regardless of where you purchased them — Mercury, Suzuki, Yamaha, Honda, and Tohatsu. Our authorised scope covers all Mercury warranty and post-warranty work. For other brands, we handle non-warranty service and repairs. Lee, Damian, and Cody perform all outboard work in our Sault Ste. Marie facility. We do not offer mobile on-water service; all work is done at the shop.

---

## Spring Commissioning

The most common reason an outboard fails at the start of the season is a neglected impeller. The water pump impeller circulates cooling water through the engine — when it fails, the engine overheats fast and the damage is immediate. Most manufacturers recommend impeller replacement every two to three seasons. If you are not certain when yours was last changed, a commissioning service will tell you.

A standard spring commissioning service covers cooling system flush, impeller inspection and replacement if needed, gear oil change, spark plug check, battery load test, fuel system inspection, and a full functional test before we return the unit. If Lee or Cody finds something beyond the commissioning scope — a cracked housing, a worn seal — Aaron will call you with a written estimate before any additional work proceeds. Most commissioning services take 2 to 4 business days once your motor is in the shop.

Book in April. Walleye season on most Algoma district inland lakes opens the third Saturday in May. The last week of April is the safe intake deadline if you want to be on the water for opening day.

---

## Ongoing Service and Repairs

Beyond seasonal service, we handle carburetor cleaning and rebuilds, fuel system diagnosis, electrical troubleshooting, lower unit seal replacements, and powerhead work. Lynn manages our parts inventory and stocks a broad range of common Mercury service parts — filters, impellers, propellers, anodes, spark plugs — with less common parts typically arriving within a few business days on order.

Not sure what is wrong? Bring it in for a diagnostic. Cody or Lee will identify the fault and provide a written estimate before any repair work begins. We do not charge a diagnostic fee if you proceed with the repair.

---

## Fall Winterisation

Leaving water in an outboard's cooling passages through a Northern Ontario winter cracks engine blocks. Sault Ste. Marie averages 290 centimetres of snowfall per year — by November, freeze-up is at the door and the damage is already done if the motor was not properly winterised in October.

Winterisation covers flushing and draining the cooling system, fogging the cylinders to protect against corrosion, stabilising the fuel, changing gear oil, and greasing all fittings. Aaron reviews each winterisation checklist before the unit is returned. Book in September or early October — by late October the schedule fills quickly and we cannot guarantee completion before the first hard freeze.
