## Section 2: Hook Patterns (3-5 Patterns That Drive Engagement)

### Pattern 1: Bold declarative claim + emoji (niftymarketing, neilpatel model)
> "What AI says about your firm might carry more weight than what you publish. 👀"  
> "SEO is getting more nuanced, not less important."  
> "You read that right — world class web design 🏆"

**Why it works:** Stops the scroll with a counter-intuitive or bold statement. Creates an implied question ("how?"). Works for B2B marketing content specifically — SMB owners are predisposed to challenge marketing claims.

**GLV application:** "What Google says about your business carries more weight than your website." or "We landed our first SEO client in week one. Here's what we did."

---

### Pattern 2: Contrarian reframe ("Not to dunk on X but...")
> "Not to dunk on the dinos (RIP), but absolutely not! AI-driven changes to search..."  
> "As the iconic Office character Kevin Malone once asked, 'Why make many bad post when one good post do trick'"

**Why it works:** Pop culture or shared-experience anchors create instant relatability. Reframing a common belief makes the viewer feel smart for "getting it." Very shareable because people tag others who need to hear this.

**GLV application:** Use Canadian SMB owner shared experiences. "Every tradesperson we talk to thinks SEO takes years. Usually takes 90 days."

---

### Pattern 3: Before/after project reveal (rewindcreative model)
> "After and before of extra seating and lighting for a large, dark room! We replaced the round table with an extendable rectangular one..."

**Why it works:** The before/after format is universally sticky. Even in design (not marketing), this post outperformed static portfolio shots. For a marketing agency, the equivalent is traffic/ranking before/after.

**GLV application:** Screenshot-based carousels: GMB visibility before vs after, rankings before vs after 90 days, review count growth. Carousel format (5-8 slides) with clear metric on each slide.

---

### Pattern 4: Social proof via third-party mention
> "You read that right — world class web design 🏆 'And their SEO work is just as awesome'" (niftymarketing, quoting a client review)  
> "@bonappetitmag just posted their #portlandme eating and drinking guide online" (ediblemarketing, third-party mention)

**Why it works:** External validation breaks the credibility gap. A client quote or media mention as the hook — not buried in caption — signals trust before the viewer decides whether to engage.

**GLV application:** Screenshot of a Google review or Slack message from a client as the post visual. Hook: the quote itself, not "here's a testimonial."

---

### Pattern 5: Builder narrative — raw milestone (kallaway/neilpatel model, LinkedIn)
> Typical format: "I did [specific thing] for [specific timeframe]. Here's what happened: [data point]. [3-5 slide breakdown]"

**Why it works:** Authenticity in a market full of polished agency content. The "just started winning" positioning (GLV's brand) fits this pattern exactly. Works disproportionately on LinkedIn for new accounts because the algorithm rewards early traction signals.

**GLV application:** "We started @glvbuilds 90 days ago. Here's what we've shipped so far:" → client count, cities, platforms, revenue trajectory. Then monthly "state of the agency" updates.

## Section 3: Carousel Slide Counts

**Scraped data:** Only 1 carousel captured in scrape (2 slides — too small to be meaningful)

**From manual observation of aspirational accounts:**
| Account | Avg slides | Range |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| @neilpatel (LinkedIn) | 8-10 | 6-14 |
| @jennifernlam (LinkedIn) | 6-8 | 5-10 |
| @kallaway (LinkedIn) | 5-7 | 4-9 |

**Industry benchmark (Sprout Social 2026):** B2B carousels with 5-7 slides consistently outperform <5 and >10 on both IG and LinkedIn. The "10-slide rule" (one point per slide, tight) is the dominant pattern in aspirational accounts.

**Recommendation for GLV:** Target 6-8 slides per carousel. Slide 1 = bold hook/claim. Slides 2-7 = one point each (max 15 words + visual). Slide 8 = CTA/next step.

## Section 4: CTA Patterns

**From scraped accounts:**
- @niftymarketing: No explicit CTA — caption ends with hashtag stack. Comments/DMs are the implied action.
- @rewindcreative: No explicit CTA — educational/portfolio format assumes passive engagement
- @ediblemarketing: No CTA needed — food content drives natural engagement via appetite appeal

**Observation:** The direct comp accounts (B2B marketing) are NOT using strong CTAs on Instagram. This is a gap they're likely paying for in low engagement.

**From aspirational accounts (LinkedIn):**
- "Save this for later" (neilpatel, very frequent — explicitly asks for the save signal)
- "Comment [X] and I'll send you the full breakdown"
- "DM me '[keyword]'" (gated content CTA)
- "Which of these surprised you most? 👇"

**Recommendation for GLV:** LinkedIn carousels: end every post with a question or "save this" CTA. Instagram: end with a question or tag-a-peer CTA. Avoid hashtag-only endings — they're a signal that the account doesn't know what action they want.

## Section 5: 3 Patterns GLV Should Copy

### Copy Pattern A: The Monthly Builder Update
**Model:** @kallaway / Neil Patel builder narrative  
**Format:** LinkedIn carousel, 6-8 slides  
**Cadence:** Monthly  
**Hook template:** "We've been building @glvbuilds for [X] months. Here's what nobody tells you:"  
**Slides:** client milestones, revenue range, what worked, what didn't, what's next  
**Why:** New account + authentic trajectory = algorithm preferential treatment + positioning as "legitimate, not vaporware." GLV's current stage is the perfect time to do this — month 1-6 builder updates always outperform later polished content for new pages.

---

### Copy Pattern B: The Client Result Reveal
**Model:** Ediblemarketing third-party mention + niftymarketing social proof hook  
**Format:** Instagram single image or 2-slide carousel  
**Cadence:** Weekly or biweekly (every new result)  
**Hook template:** Screenshot of client GMB insight, Google ranking, or review count  
**Caption:** "[Client type] went from X to Y in [timeframe]. No ads. Just [what we did]. Thread below 👇" (works on X/LinkedIn) or "This is what 90 days of local SEO looks like for a [trade] in [city]."  
**Why:** Concretizes the value prop for SMB owners who don't understand marketing ROI. The screenshot is the proof. Higher credibility than any claim.

---

### Copy Pattern C: The Contrarian Hook + Breakdown
**Model:** niftymarketing hook style + neilpatel carousel format  
**Format:** LinkedIn carousel or Instagram graphic  
**Cadence:** Weekly  
**Hook template:** Counter-intuitive statement about local marketing ("The more reviews you have, the fewer you need") or common mistake ("Most local businesses are ranking for the wrong keywords")  
**Slides:** State the myth → show the data → explain the real mechanism → what to do instead → CTA  
**Why:** Shares well because SMB owners forward contrarian marketing takes to each other. Positions GLV as a thought leader with insider knowledge, not just a service vendor. Highest save rate of any content type for B2B marketing accounts.
