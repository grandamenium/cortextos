## Section 1: Meta CPL Benchmarks — Real Estate / Housing Verticals (CAD)

### 1.1 Canada Real Estate CPL — SuperAds.ai (Primary Source)

**Source:** [SuperAds.ai — Facebook Ads Cost Benchmarks for Real Estate in Canada (2025)](https://www.superads.ai/facebook-ads-costs/cost-per-lead/real-estate/canada)
**Data set:** $3B+ in advertising data
**Date coverage:** January 2025 – January 2026
**Confidence:** HIGH (large sample, Canada-specific, time-series)

| Metric | Value (USD reported) | CAD est. (×1.38) |
|--------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Jan 2025 CPL | $83 | ~$115 CAD |
| Dec 2025 CPL | $63 | ~$87 CAD |
| Full-year 2025 average | $124 | ~$171 CAD |
| Global average (same dataset) | $41 | ~$57 CAD |
| Canada vs global premium | ~3× global | — |

**Key volatility pattern:**
- Peak: August 2025 — Canada was 11.7× the global CPL benchmark
- Trough: September 2025 — Canada was 39% *below* global benchmark
- Q1 avg: $56; Q2 avg: $125 (April spike); Q3 avg: $192 (August outlier at $505); Q4 avg: $123
- Jan 2026 retraced sharply after the December 2025 peak

**IMPORTANT NOTE:** These numbers are far higher than the prior baseline's $30–$65 CAD estimate. Two reconciling factors:
1. SuperAds data likely captures all real estate including luxury residential and commercial — not just small-builder/housing
2. The August 2025 outlier ($505 USD) inflates the annual average dramatically — remove it and the picture normalizes
3. The March and September troughs ($35–$45 range) are more consistent with the prior proxy

**Revised interpretation:** The $63–$83 USD endpoint range (Dec 2025 / Jan 2025) is the most operationally relevant. At $63 USD ≈ $87 CAD in December 2025, this is the current realistic ceiling for Canadian real estate lead gen. The prior $30–$65 CAD proxy may be optimistic for fully unoptimized campaigns but reasonable for post-learning, well-structured lead form campaigns.

- Canada CPM range: $9.15 (Jan 2026 low) – $20.94 (Dec 2025 high)

- Canada CPMs surged +122% over 2025 before retracing in Jan 2026

**Source:** [AdAmigo.ai — Meta Ads CPM by Country 2026](https://www.adamigo.ai/blog/meta-ads-cpm-cpc-benchmarks-by-country-2026)

- Canada classified as Tier 1 market (per AdAmigo taxonomy — US, Australia, Canada, Western Europe)
- Tier 1 CPM range: **$10–$23 USD** (~$14–$32 CAD)
- 2025→2026 Tier 1 CPM increase: +12% YoY
- 2026 cross-industry average CPM: **$13.48 USD** (~$18.60 CAD), up 20% from 2025

### 2.2 Tier-2 Ontario vs GTA / Toronto CPM Gap

**CRITICAL GAP: No published data found.**

There is no public benchmark that breaks Canadian Meta CPM down by city or DMA equivalent. Available data is country-level only. The following is inference from structural principles:

| Factor | Directional impact on Tier-2 CPM vs GTA |
|--------|----------------------------------------|
| Smaller addressable audience (SSM pop. ~75K vs GTA 6M+) | HIGHER CPM (less auction competition per impression) |
| Fewer advertisers bidding on SSM-area users | LOWER CPM (thinner auction) |
| Lower disposable income in market (less attractive to national brands) | LOWER CPM |
| Broad Ontario-wide targeting (Andromeda) bypasses city-level differentiation | CPM closer to provincial average |

**Net assessment:** Tier-2 CPM is likely 15–35% *lower* than GTA CPM when using city-level geo targeting, due to thinner advertiser competition. However, when using Ontario-wide broad targeting (Andromeda strategy), the CPM blends toward the provincial average (~$14–$18 CAD). This is the intended behavior — Andromeda finds the cheapest inventory to hit the conversion signal, regardless of geography.

**CTR benchmark (national):** 2.19% median across Meta per vaizle.com (2026 data); most industries 2%–3%.

**This gap remains unfilled by public data.** No published source quantifies SSM / Sudbury / Thunder Bay vs GTA CPM differential on Meta.

## Section 3: Meta Advantage+ / Andromeda Impact on CPL (2025–2026)

### 3.1 Andromeda Engine Basics

**Source:** [IMM Digital — Unpacking Meta's 2025 Ad Overhaul: Andromeda, Advantage+](https://imm.com/blog/unpacking-meta-2025-ad-overhaul-andromeda-advantage-and-what-it-means-for-your-ads/)
**Date:** 2025
**Confidence:** HIGH (structural change, widely corroborated)

- Andromeda replaced Facebook's delivery engine between late 2024 and early 2025
- Uses NVIDIA Grace Hopper Superchip for real-time per-user ad ranking
- Prioritizes engagement quality and conversion signal clarity over audience segmentation
- Creative is now the primary targeting lever; interest stacking is deprioritized

### 3.2 Andromeda / Advantage+ CPL Impact — Quantified

**Source:** Multiple (Meta internal benchmarks relayed via dancingchicken.com, adnabu.com, enrichlabs.ai)
**Date:** 2025–2026
**Confidence:** MEDIUM (Meta-sourced figures; not independently audited)

| Metric | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Meta internal: Advantage+ vs manual campaigns | Up to **10% lower CPL** |
| Advantage+ creative (1 ad set, 25 creatives) | **17% more conversions at 16% lower cost** |
| Advantage+ ROAS lift | +22% |
| Advantage+ CPA reduction (Meta claim, e-commerce+lead gen) | Up to **32% lower CPA** |
| Lead generation objective specifically | **9.7% lower cost per result** |
| Reduction in cost per qualified lead | **10%** |

**Important nuance (leadgen-economy.com, 2026):**
- 21% CPL *spike* documented in 2026 for lead-objective campaigns — this is NOT contradicted by the above; it reflects what happens when Advantage+ is used *without* downstream signal (no CAPI, no CRM feedback)
- Campaigns without CRM-CAPI feedback loop saw CPL rise 21% while conversion rates fell
- Campaigns WITH CRM-CAPI and 40–50 qualified/closed-won events/month flowing back to Meta reported positive Advantage+ Leads results

**Source:** [LeadGen Economy — Facebook Lead Ads Quality Collapse in 2026](https://www.leadgen-economy.com/blog/facebook-lead-ads-quality-collapse-cpl/)

**Conclusion for Titan:** At $500/mo with no CAPI loop, Advantage+ broad targeting will likely see CPL at or above industry average. The 10–32% improvement numbers require either (a) strong creative volume (25+ variants) or (b) active CAPI feedback. Neither is available at launch. Plan for mid-range CPL, not the optimistic end.

### 3.3 Andromeda Creative Requirements

**Source:** [Logical Position — 2026 Paid Social Playbook](https://www.logicalposition.com/blog/the-2026-paid-social-playbook)

- Local businesses: location-focused creative (landmarks, local language, community references) performs best with broad targeting
- Testimonial videos from satisfied clients outperform generic housing imagery
- 25 creative variants per ad set is the stated Meta recommendation for Advantage+
- At Titan's budget, aim for 3–5 strong variants minimum; more is better

## Section 4: Lead Form vs. Landing Page CPL Differential

### 4.1 Quantified Differential

**Source:** [AdAmigo.ai — Meta Lead Form vs Landing Page Benchmarks by Industry (2026)](https://www.adamigo.ai/blog/meta-lead-form-vs-landing-page-benchmarks-by-industry-2026)
**Date:** 2026
**Confidence:** HIGH (industry-wide, well-corroborated across multiple sources)

| Format | CPL relative to baseline | Notes |
|--------|--------------------------|-------|
| Meta Instant Form (lead form) | **30–70% lower CPL** vs landing page | Pre-filled from Meta profile; zero friction |
| Landing page (external URL) | Baseline | Higher friction; lower conversion rate |
| Video ad → lead form | Avg $34.10 USD (~$47 CAD) | Per housing vertical data |
| Video ad → landing page | Avg $45.80 USD (~$63 CAD) | Per housing vertical data |

**Specific differential in housing:** Lead form is ~25% cheaper than landing page in the housing category (based on the $34.10 vs $45.80 video ad data point).

The 30–70% range reflects across all industries; housing/real estate with high-consideration buyers likely sits at the conservative end: **30–40% lower CPL with lead form vs landing page.**

### 4.2 Quality Trade-off

- Lead forms: lower CPL but lower intent — leads are pre-filled, some won't remember submitting
- Landing pages: higher CPL but higher intent — user actively typed info, indicating genuine interest
- For tiny homes ($100K–$150K purchase): landing page quality advantage is meaningful
- Recommendation: lead form for volume in learning phase; add landing page A/B test at Month 2+ if lead quality is poor

## Section 5: Learning Phase Exit — Budget & Conversion Threshold

### 5.1 Standard Threshold

**Source:** [Meta Business Help Center — About the Learning Phase](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/112167992830700); [AdStellar — Meta Ads Learning Phase Explained 2026](https://www.adstellar.ai/blog/meta-ads-learning-phase-explained)
**Date:** 2025–2026
**Confidence:** HIGH (official Meta documentation + corroborated)

- **Standard threshold:** 50 optimization events per ad set per week
- **Some 2025–2026 reports:** 10 conversions over 3 days as alternative threshold (down from 50 over 7 days in some campaign structures)
- **Budget calculation:** If target CPL = $50 CAD ($36 USD) and need 50 events/week → need ~7 conversions/day → need ~$252 USD/day minimum (~$350 CAD/day)
- **$500/month budget = ~$16.67 CAD/day** — structurally impossible to exit learning phase by standard threshold

### 5.2 Budget Guidance for Housing Vertical

**Source:** [Code3 — Understanding the Meta Learning Phase](https://code3.com/resources/understanding-the-meta-learning-phase-why-it-matters-for-campaign-performance/); [AdStellar learning phase guides](https://www.adstellar.ai/blog/meta-ads-learning-phase-struggles)

- Home services / real estate recommended daily minimum: **$150–$300 USD/day** (~$207–$414 CAD/day)
- At $16.67/day: campaign will likely remain in learning phase for the duration of Month 1
- With 10–20 conversions/week at $500/mo budget, exit will take **2–4 weeks minimum** — possibly never

### 5.3 Practical Implications for Titan ($500/mo)

| Scenario | Implication |
|----------|-------------|
| Standard 50 conv/week threshold | Unreachable at $500/mo |
| 10 conversions/week (lower informal threshold) | Possible in weeks 3–5 if CPL is $45–$55 CAD |
| Budget increase resets learning | Any >20% budget change resets — avoid mid-month changes |
| CBO with 1–2 broad ad sets | Consolidates signal; best chance of exit at low budget |
| Optimize for higher-funnel event | Consider "Lead" event via CAPI vs just form submit — better signal quality |

**Recommended stance:** Treat Month 1 entirely as learning phase. Do not optimize or kill ad sets before Day 30. Compare CPL at Day 30 vs proxy range, not Day 14.

### 5.4 2025–2026 Changes to Learning Phase

**Source:** [Dataslayer — Every Meta Ads Change in 2025-2026](https://www.dataslayer.ai/blog/meta-ads-changes-2025-83-updates-that-changed-facebook-advertising-forever)

- Meta now recommends 7+ days before making any changes (up from 3–4 days)
- Meta has started showing specific budget increase allowances ("You can increase your budget to $179 without restarting learning") — first time they've quantified this
- Do NOT increase budget >20% in a single change; increase 20% → wait 3–4 days → increase 20% again if needed

The closest proxy is the national average (~$14–$18 CAD CPM). Tier-2 is directionally cheaper when geo-targeted (less competition), but no number can be cited with confidence.

## May 11 Refresh — New Findings (pass date: 2026-05-11)

### HIGH PRIORITY — Actionable at Stage 4 Campaign Setup

**1. Advantage+ Creative defaults ON for all new Lead campaigns (since Feb 2026)**
Source: SocialBee (May 8, 2026) + Prism ME | Confidence: HIGH

Since February 2026, all new campaigns under the Leads objective launch with ALL Advantage+ Creative enhancements pre-selected. Meta will automatically apply visual modifications (cropping, aspect ratio changes, background generation, text variations, music overlays) to ad assets unless explicitly disabled per enhancement.

Titan implication: UGC creative authenticity is the primary creative strategy — unmodified phone-shot Joey/Kathryn clips. Automatic Advantage+ Creative modifications risk undermining that authenticity. At Stage 4 setup, explicitly review and selectively disable per enhancement. Recommended approach:
- DISABLE: Background generation, music overlays, image expansion (for video)
- EVALUATE: Text variations, image brightness/contrast (low risk)
- KEEP: Aspect ratio adjustments for placement fit

**2. Housing Special Ad Category — 15-mile minimum radius; age/gender targeting prohibited**
Source: Mediastrobe/Medium (March 2026) | Confidence: HIGH

Under Meta's Housing Special Ad Category (which applies to real estate and residential construction), geo-targeting requires a minimum 15-mile radius. Age and gender targeting are prohibited. Postal code targeting is not available.

Titan implications:
- Ontario-wide targeting is compliant AND is the only compliant strategy (no SSM-only, no GTA-exclusion, no age 28-65 targeting). This aligns with the existing Andromeda broad-targeting strategy.
- Ad Set B (Downsizer — age 50-70) is NOT compliant under Housing Special Ad Category. Age targeting is prohibited. Must redesign Ad Set B to use creative differentiation only, not age targeting.
- Campaign must be filed under "Housing" Special Ad Category at setup. Failure to do so risks ad rejection.

### MEDIUM PRIORITY — Informational / Deferred

**3. AI-generated content disclosure label now required (since March 2026)**
Source: Dataslayer | Confidence: HIGH

Meta requires a disclosure label on any ad containing AI-generated or AI-modified visuals. Applies even to AI-enhanced photos (background removal, upscaling). UGC shot on-site is not affected. Flag at creative review if any AI-assisted assets are in the bundle.

**4. "Describe Your Audience" natural language targeting input in Advantage+**
Source: Prism ME | Confidence: MEDIUM (rollout not confirmed fleet-wide)

Advertisers can now write a plain-English description of their ideal customer inside Advantage+ Targeting. Richer signal than interest category dropdowns. Test at Month 2 if available in the account: "Ontario homeowners interested in downsizing, sustainable living, alternative housing, or off-grid lifestyle."

**5. Three new Andromeda creative KPI metrics now in Ads Manager**
Source: Uproas, AdScale | Confidence: MEDIUM (metric names not publicly documented)

Platform-native metrics now signal when a creative library is stale and needs new content. Monitor in Ads Manager at Month 1 review rather than relying solely on CPL trend.

**6. Advantage+ Shopping conversion threshold reduced: 50 → 25 events/week**
Source: Prism ME | Confidence: HIGH (but applies to Shopping objective only)

Applies specifically to Advantage+ Shopping campaigns; Leads objective threshold not confirmed changed. Not actionable at $500/mo (still structurally unable to reach 25 conv/week). Worth monitoring if Titan ever tests a sales-conversion objective.
